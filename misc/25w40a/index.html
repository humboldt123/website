Create any shape with straight edges by folding and making a single cut. This interactive visualization demonstrates the fold-and-cut theorem, which states that it is possible to fold a piece of flat material and make a single straight complete cut, such that when unfolded, the material is cut into any desired polygonal shape.

This demo is inspired by the paper "Folding and One Straight Cut Suffice" by Erik D. Demaine, Martin L. Demaine, and Anna Lubiw. Read the paper here.
